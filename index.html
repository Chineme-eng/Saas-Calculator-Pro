SaaS founder reminders
01
Churn kills quietly
You can add users every month and still stall if churn keeps erasing your growth. Fix retention before scaling acquisition.
02
Price is leverage
A small increase in price can move MRR faster than chasing the same number of extra users. Test pricing early and often.
03
LTV must beat CAC
If your customer is not worth much more than the cost to acquire them, scale gets dangerous fast. Target 3× LTV/CAC minimum.
04
NRR over 100% is a superpower
When existing customers expand faster than others churn, you grow revenue without new users. This is what investors love most.
05
Rule of 40 matters to investors
Growth rate % + profit margin % should exceed 40%. It shows you're balancing speed and efficiency. Below 20 is a red flag at scale.
06
CAC payback window
If it takes 18+ months to recover your acquisition cost, you need lower CAC or higher price before scaling paid channels.
07
Cohort retention compounds
Even 2% less monthly churn dramatically improves how much revenue a cohort generates over its lifetime. Small improvements matter.
08
Sensitivity before scaling
Before spending on growth, test your model across price and churn combinations. Small changes flip unit economics entirely.
09
Expansion revenue changes everything
Even 2–3% monthly expansion from upgrades and upsells can push NRR above 100%, making churn irrelevant at the portfolio level.
10
Know your runway always
Founders who don't know their runway to the month make bad decisions under pressure. Keep a live model and update it monthly.
